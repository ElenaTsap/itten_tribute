Johannes Itten was a Swiss expressionist painter, designer, teacher, writer and theorist associated with the Bauhaus school.

1888

Born in Südern-Linden, Switzerland.

1904

Αttended a teaching course in Hofwil near Bern till 1906.

1908

Worked as a primary school teacher.

1909

Registered at the École des Beaux-Arts in Geneva and studied there until 1910.

1912

Completed another degree in natural science and mathematics at the University of Bern and received his diploma as a secondary school teacher. At the same period he studied at the Stuttgart Academy and became a member of Adolf Hölzel’s master students’ studio till 1914.

1916

First solo exhibition dedicated to Itten’s work was organised by Herwarth Walden at his gallery Der Sturm in Berlin. That same year, Itten relocated to Vienna and opened a private art school there.

1919

Was appointed as one of the first masters at the Staatliches Bauhaus in Weimar by Walter Gropius. he was both director of the preliminary course which he had developed independently for the introductory semester and master of form of all the workshops except for the ceramic, bookbinding and printing workshops. Itten made a significant contribution to the Bauhaus by promoting the Mazdaznan cult, which spans religions and philosophies.

1923

After internal differences with Walter Gropius, Itten left the Bauhaus

1926

Was appointed to direct the newly established Höhere Fachschule für Textile Flächenkunst (advanced school of textile art) in Krefeld.

1932

Was appointed to direct the newly established Höhere Fachschule für Textile Flächenkunst (advanced school of textile art) in Krefeld.

1934

The Itten school in Berlin was closed by the NSDAP

1937

Itten’s work was displayed at the exhibition Entartete Kunst (degenerate art) in Munich. He was dismissed from his post at the academy in Krefeld. Itten then moved to the Netherlands.

1938

Became the director of the Kunstgewerbeschule (school of applied arts) and the Kunstgewerbemuseum (museum of applied arts) in Zurich.

1943

Became director of the Textilfachschule (textiles school) in Zurich.

1949

He was commissioned to design the Rietberg Museum for non-European art in Zurich.

1955

He was invited to join the Ulm School of Design (HfG) by Max Bill.

1957

Large retrospectives of his work were held at, among others, at the Stedelijk Museum in Amsterdam.

1964

Large retrospectives of his work were held at, among others, at the Kunsthaus Zürich.

1967

Died in Zurich
